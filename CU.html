Context

During the time I studied at UC Riverside, I came up with the hypothesis that design is a ‚Äúnudge‚Äù, which means to guide, but this was difficult to see because of the restraints from business and technology. However, I was determined to test my hypothesis. Project Brief

ChineseUnion is a student organization which aims to support all international students. In this project, we focus on providing newly arrived students with developed pickup service and derivative services, such as card-issuing service, and hotel service.


Time: July 2019 - Sep 2019


Roles: Product Manager and UX Designer

My contribution

1. Define problems and integrate business goal with user needs
2. Organize and led multiple user research and conduct user experience design
3. Build up and lead the cross functional team

Problem

In 2019, there were 369,548 Chinese students who came to the U.S. pursuing higher education. The first challenge they faced is the feeling of helplessness and strangeness in the airport because they were not familiar with local ways of doing business.

Research

Based on our 6 years pickup experience and direct observation, we have found that a number of students have difficulty finding the way to the meeting area. What‚Äôs more is that, based on our contextual inquiry with a lot of students, the result shows that International students have two or more baggage items, which are burdensome and bulky. This was especially a big challenge to most female respondents.


During my phone interview with 16 interviewees, 9 interviewees reported that they felt nervous during the process especially before they passed customs. Only 3 of 16 were confident in speaking English to strangers.


We also brainstormed during meetings and found that most students are only children, and their parents worry when their children go abroad. So, students are used to contacting their parents immediately after arriving.
